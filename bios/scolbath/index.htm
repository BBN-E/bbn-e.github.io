Sean Colbath

 

Sean Colbath is a Lead Scientist in the Analytics and Machine Intelligence department. Joining BBN in 1994, Sean has focused on the transition of human language-focused machine learning capabilities to operational use, including machine translation, speech recognition, and other core capabilities that leverage AMI’s core DARPA and IARPA research.

For the last decade, Sean has managed the AMI Media Exploitation Solutions business, which includes which includes our Multimedia Monitoring System (M3S) and the Machine Foreign Language Translation System (MFLTS) program of record with the U.S. Army. M3S collects and translates media ranging from live television to web-based and social media in over 18 different languages, making it searchable and comprehensible by English-language speakers. M3S is currently deployed at operationally at USSOCOM and USCENTCOM, and its predecessor capabilities have been used by the US Government and other foreign governments at home and around the world. The US Army MFLTS program of record focused on speech-to-speech translation, enabling a monolingual English-speaking soldier to converse with and engage in functional conversations with a non-English-speaking foreign national. This system, called TransTalk, runs entirely on an Android phone and has been deployed operationally on over 30,000 phones in Iraq and Afghanistan since 2016.

Sean has served in several leadership roles at BBN, including team leader, principal investigator, technical lead, chief architect and business developer. He holds a bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from the University of Rochester, and is a senior member of the IEEE. Sean also has six U.S. patents, including one for a voice-activated web browser.
